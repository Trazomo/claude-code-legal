Draft a legal research memo on $ARGUMENTS using IRAC format.

## Structure

### Issue
State the legal question precisely. Frame it as a yes/no or "whether" question.

### Rule
Cite governing authority using Bluebook 21st Edition format:
- Lead with the controlling statute or regulation
- Follow with binding case law from the relevant jurisdiction
- Note any circuit splits or conflicting authority

### Analysis
Apply the rule to our facts:
- Address the strongest counterargument head-on
- Distinguish unfavorable precedent where possible
- Identify factual gaps that need investigation
- Note any assumptions made

### Conclusion
Provide a clear recommendation with confidence level:
- "Likely to prevail" / "Uncertain — further investigation needed" / "Significant risk"
- If uncertain, identify what additional facts or research would resolve the question

## Defaults
- **Jurisdiction**: New York unless specified otherwise
- **Citation Format**: Bluebook 21st Edition
- **Audience**: Senior partner (assume sophisticated legal reader)

## Important
- Always flag if this is an unsettled area of law
- Verify all citations exist — do not fabricate case names or reporter citations
- Note any ethical issues (conflicts, privilege, competence) immediately
