# Legal Memo Conventions

These rules load automatically when drafting or reviewing legal memoranda.

## Format: IRAC

All legal memos use IRAC structure:

1. **Issue** — State the precise legal question. One issue per section.
2. **Rule** — Cite the governing authority:
   - Lead with the statute or regulation
   - Follow with controlling case law
   - Note any circuit splits or unsettled areas
3. **Analysis** — Apply the rule to our facts:
   - Address the strongest counterargument
   - Distinguish unfavorable precedent
   - Note factual gaps that need investigation
4. **Conclusion** — Clear recommendation with confidence level:
   - "Likely prevail" / "Uncertain" / "Significant risk"

## Citation Format

Bluebook 21st Edition. Examples:
- *Ashcroft v. Iqbal*, 556 U.S. 662, 678 (2009)
- N.Y. C.P.L.R. section 3211(a)(7) (McKinney 2024)
- Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6)

## Jurisdiction Check
- Verify all cited cases are from the correct jurisdiction
- Prefer binding authority over persuasive authority
- If using out-of-jurisdiction cases, explain why (e.g., no controlling authority in NY)

## Unsettled Law
- Always flag if the area of law is unsettled or evolving
- Note any pending legislation or recent regulatory changes
- If a circuit split exists, identify which position favors the client

## Conflicts
- Flag any potential conflicts of interest identified during research
- Note if the analysis could adversely affect another client matter
