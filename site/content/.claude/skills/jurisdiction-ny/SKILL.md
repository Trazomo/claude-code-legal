# New York Jurisdiction Reference

## Statutes of Limitation (CPLR Article 2)

| Cause of Action | Period | Authority |
|-----------------|--------|-----------|
| Breach of contract (written) | 6 years | CPLR section 213(2) |
| Breach of contract (oral) | 6 years | CPLR section 213(2) |
| Fraud | 6 years (or 2 years from discovery) | CPLR section 213(8), section 203(g) |
| Negligence / Personal injury | 3 years | CPLR section 214(5) |
| Medical malpractice | 2 years 6 months | CPLR section 214-a |
| Property damage | 3 years | CPLR section 214(4) |
| Legal malpractice | 3 years | CPLR section 214(6) |
| Products liability | 3 years | CPLR section 214(5) |

## Statute of Frauds — GOL section 5-701

Contracts that **must** be in writing under NY General Obligations Law section 5-701:
- Agreements not to be performed within one year
- Promises to answer for the debt of another
- Agreements in consideration of marriage
- Real property contracts (also Real Property Law section 5-703)
- Contracts for sale of goods over $500 (UCC section 2-201)

## Business Corporation Law — Formation

Required elements for NY corporation formation:
1. Certificate of Incorporation filed with Secretary of State
2. Registered agent designated in NY
3. Publication requirement (NY Bus. Corp. Law section 304) — two newspapers, 6 consecutive weeks
4. Biennial statement filing with DOS

## UCC Article 2 — Sale of Goods

NY has adopted UCC Article 2 for sale of goods. Key thresholds:
- Writing required for contracts over $500 (section 2-201)
- Perfect tender rule applies (section 2-601)
- Merchant's firm offer (section 2-205) — irrevocable for stated time, max 3 months

## Citation Format for NY Courts

- Court of Appeals: *Wood v. Lucy, Lady Duff-Gordon*, 222 N.Y. 88 (1917)
- Appellate Division: *Smith v. Jones*, 100 A.D.3d 500 (1st Dep't 2022)
- Supreme Court: *Doe v. Roe*, 50 Misc. 3d 100 (Sup. Ct. N.Y. Cnty. 2023)
- NY Supplement: Include N.Y.S.3d parallel citation when available
