# Key New York Cases — Quick Reference

Frequently cited NY cases for common legal issues. All citations in Bluebook format.

## Contract Law

**Implied Promises / Good Faith**
*Wood v. Lucy, Lady Duff-Gordon*, 222 N.Y. 88 (1917)
— A promise may be implied from the entire circumstances of a contract, even if not expressly stated. Cardozo, J.

**Substantial Performance**
*Jacob & Youngs, Inc. v. Kent*, 230 N.Y. 239 (1921)
— Substantial performance satisfies a contract obligation where the defect is minor and unintentional. Damages measured by diminution in value, not cost of replacement. Cardozo, J.

**Unconscionability**
*Williams v. Walker-Thomas Furniture Co.*, 350 F.2d 445 (D.C. Cir. 1965)
— Cross-collateral clause in consumer contract held unconscionable. (Persuasive in NY courts.)

## Torts / Negligence

**Proximate Cause / Foreseeability**
*Palsgraf v. Long Island R.R. Co.*, 248 N.Y. 339 (1928)
— Negligence requires a duty to the particular plaintiff; liability limited to foreseeable consequences. Cardozo, C.J.

**Duty to Rescue**
*Strauss v. Belle Realty Co.*, 65 N.Y.2d 399 (1985)
— No duty owed to non-customers of a utility; privity-like limitation on duty.

## Civil Procedure

**Notice Pleading Standard (NY)**
*Leon v. Martinez*, 84 N.Y.2d 83 (1994)
— On motion to dismiss under CPLR 3211(a)(7), court must accept facts as alleged and determine whether they fit within any cognizable legal theory.

**Summary Judgment**
*Alvarez v. Prospect Hosp.*, 68 N.Y.2d 320 (1986)
— Movant must make a prima facie showing of entitlement to judgment as a matter of law. Failure to do so requires denial regardless of opposition papers.

## Business Law

**Piercing the Corporate Veil**
*Morris v. N.Y. State Dep't of Taxation & Fin.*, 82 N.Y.2d 135 (1993)
— Requires showing that the owner exercised complete domination and that such domination was used to commit a fraud or wrong.

**Business Judgment Rule**
*Auerbach v. Bennett*, 47 N.Y.2d 619 (1979)
— Courts will not second-guess good-faith business decisions of disinterested directors.
